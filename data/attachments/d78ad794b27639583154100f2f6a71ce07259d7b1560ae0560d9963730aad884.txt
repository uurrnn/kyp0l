WHEREAS,  the Board  of  Zoning Adjustment  does  find that  the  Plan  2040 Community 
Form  Goal  1  Policy  12  states  that  the  design  of  parking,  loading  and  delivery  areas 
located adjacent to residential uses should minimize adverse impacts from light, noise, 
and other potential impacts. It also calls to ensure that these parking, loading, and delivery 
areas are convenient for motorists and does not negatively impact nearby residents or 
pedestrians. Community Form Goal 1 Policy 17 states that development must mitigate 
adverse  impacts  of  traffic  on  nearby  existing  communities.  There  is  a  residential  use 
directly  to  the  west  of  the  development  site,  therefore,  the  proposed  site  design,  with 
parking in front of the structure, will serve to minimize operational and visual impacts of 
the  land  use  while  providing  safer  and  more  direct  vehicular  access  to  the  two  street 
frontages, and  

WHEREAS, the Board of Zoning Adjustment does find that the extent of the waiver of the 
regulation is the minimum necessary to afford relief to the applicant. Due to the size and 
layout of the proposed site, and orientation on a street corner, the applicant is limited on 
where parking spaces can be placed. Locating the parking with easy and direct access 
to  a  major  arterial  and  away  from  the  residual  use  makes  the  most  sense  when 
considering the impacts of the land use, and  

WHEREAS, the Board of Zoning Adjustment finds the strict application of the provisions 
of  the  regulation  would  create  an  unnecessary  hardship  on  the  applicant  as  it  would 
require a complete redesign of the site. Additionally, adjacent residential properties could 
be negatively impacted if the parking was required to be located in the rear, now, therefore 
be it.

WHEREAS, the Board of Zoning Adjustment does find the requested variance arises from 
special circumstances which do not generally apply to land in the general vicinity or the 
same zone. The subject site is a commercially zoned corner lot adjacent to residential 
zoning,  which  requires  compliance  with  residential  (R-4)  street  side  yard  standards, 
including  more  restrictive  fence  height  limitations.  While  a  variance  was  previously 
approved to allow the structure within the street side yard, the application of residential 
fencing standards to a building-integrated screening wall creates a unique condition not 
typical of similarly zoned commercial properties, and  

WHEREAS,  the  Board  of  Zoning  Adjustment  does  find  the  strict  application  of  the 
provisions of the regulation would create an unnecessary hardship for the applicant, as 
limiting the screening wall to a height of four feet would not provide adequate separation 
between the patio and the adjacent parking area. The wall is designed as a continuation 
of the principal structure and functions as an integrated architectural element rather than 
a  typical  fence.  Requiring  full  compliance  would  diminish  the  functionality  of  the  patio 
space and require modification to an already constructed feature, despite the previously 
approved variance allowing the structure within the street side yard, and

Land Development Code Section 4.2.63(D) and will require relief from this provision, as

area,  staff  does  not  believe  that  relief  is  justified  because  approval  would  result  in  an 
overconcentration of short term rentals, now, therefore be it.
