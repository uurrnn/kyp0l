CASE NO. 25-VARIANCE-0129 

Variance  from  the  St.  Matthews  Development  Code  Section  4.12.C.2.d  to  allow  a 
parking lot to encroach into the rear yard setback. 
Rear yard setback (Requirement 30’, Request 10’, Variance 20’)  

01:44:00 
On  a  motion  by  Member  Rodriguez,  seconded  by  Member  Lewis,  the 
following  resolution,  based  on  the  staff  report,  staff  analysis,  and  the  evidence  and 
testimony heard today, was adopted:  

WHEREAS,  the Board  of  Zoning  Adjustment  does find  the  requested  variance  will  not 
adversely  affect  the public  health, safety, or  welfare, because  the request  to encroach 
into the required setback does not impede the safe movement of vehicles or pedestrians 
along  any  right-of-way.  Additionally,  the  applicant  is  proposing  to  construct  a  7  foot 
privacy  fence  and  6  foot  landscape  buffer  along  the  northern  property  line  to  mitigate 
potential impacts from vehicles in the proposed parking lot, and 

WHEREAS,  the Board  of  Zoning  Adjustment  does find  the  requested  variance  will  not 
alter the essential character of the general vicinity as the location of the proposed parking 
is  situated  between  the  proposed  structures  and  landscape  buffer  areas  along  the 
northern  property  line.  Multi-family  developments  in  the  general  vicinity  have  parking 
located in the side and rear yards, and 

WHEREAS, the Board of Zoning Adjustment does find the requested variance arises from 
circumstances which do not generally apply to land in the general vicinity or in the same 
zone. Due to the layout of the subject property, the site has double frontage on Westport 
Road and Ridgeway Avenue which causes the site to have two rear yards. If the road 
frontage was limited to Ridgeway Avenue, the northern property line would be considered 
a side yard which requires a 10 foot setback, and 

WHEREAS,  the  Board  of  Zoning  Adjustment  does  find  the  strict  application  of  the 
provision of the regulation would deprive the applicant of the reasonable use of the land 
as the applicant is proposing to mitigate potential impacts from vehicles parking on-site 
by providing a 7 foot privacy fence along the northern property line that abuts the adjoining 
single-family residential properties, and 

WHEREAS,  the  Board  of  Zoning  Adjustment  does  find  the  circumstances  are  not  the 
result of action of the applicant taken subsequent to the adoption of the zoning regulation 
from which relief is sought as work has not begun on the proposed development, now, 
therefore be it.

On a motion by Member Lewis, seconded by Member Horton, the following 
resolution, based on the staff report, staff analysis, and the evidence and testimony heard 
today, was adopted:  

WHEREAS, the Board of Zoning Adjustment does find the approval of a freestanding sign 
that  exceeds  the  permitted  height  and  area  along  Dixie  Highway  would  allow  an 
unreasonable  circumvention  of  the  zoning  regulations.  Other  recently  developed 
commercial uses along the arterial corridor, including offices, banks, and schools, have 
complied with the established sign standards. The presence of two larger nonconforming 
signs  in  the  area  does  not  constitute  sufficient  justification  to  exceed  the  current 
requirements. Additionally, the posted speed limit of 35 miles per hour along this corridor 
does  not  warrant  increased  sign  height  beyond  that  permitted  by  the  Shively  Land 
Development Code, and 

WHEREAS, the Board of Zoning Adjustment does find that the requested variance does 
not arise from special circumstances unique to the subject property. The lot configuration, 
corner location, and arterial frontage are conditions common to other properties within the 
same zoning district and corridor. As such, the circumstances cited by the applicant are 
generally applicable to similarly situated properties and do not justify deviation from the 
established standards, and
