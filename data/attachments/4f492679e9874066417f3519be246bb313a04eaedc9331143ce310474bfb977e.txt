WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the proposal meets Plan 2040 Community 
Form:  Goal  1,  Objectives  b.,  c.,  and  f.,  because  the  proposed  zoning  districts  are 
compatible  with  existing  residential  characteristics  in  neighborhoods  that  are  currently

that  once  contained  residences  and 
lots 
neighborhood serving commercial uses. Future growth and development patterns shall 
foster  health  and  prosperity  for  all  neighborhoods.  The  current  zoning  district  permits 
heavy industrial uses that could adversely affect the health and prosperity of residences 
who  have  been  disproportionately  impacted  by  industrial  expansion.  The  proposed 
changes in zoning would promote infill development, revitalization, and adaptive reuse of 
existing structures and vacant land. 

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
1, Policy 6 because the Comprehensive Plan discourages non-residential expansion into 
existing residential areas unless it is demonstrated that adverse impacts will be mitigated 
and evaluated in the context of how the intensity of the proposed development will impact 
existing  residential  communities.  Most  of  the  subject  properties  are  located  within 
residential  communities  and  neighborhoods  surrounded  by  residential  and  commercial 
uses  that  are  significantly  less  intense  than  heavy  commercial  and  industrial  uses 
permitted  in  EZ-1.  The  Enterprise  Zone  district  contains  regulations  that  encourage 
inequitable  development  and  exacerbates  environmental  injustice  through  relaxed 
standards  for  industrial  uses.  The  inequities  of  intense  industry  in  close  proximity  to 
residential uses could be perpetuated if the Landbank-owned properties were returned to 
private ownership within the EZ-1 zoning district. 

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
1,  Policy  8  because  the  Comprehensive  Plan  encourages  industries  to  locate  in 
Workplace Form Districts or in areas readily served by infrastructure and transportation 
facilities, including transit, rather than isolated industrial sites. New industrial development 
of  the  Landbank-owned  properties  would  result  in  isolated  industrial  sites  that  are 
surrounded  by  lower  intensity,  mostly  residential,  land  uses.  Although  most  of  the 
residential  properties  are  within  the  Traditional  Workplace  form  district,  the  form  is 
inconsistent with the historic and existing land uses of the properties.

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
1, Policies 15 & 16 because the Comprehensive Plan implores considering the impacts 
of heavy industry such as potentially nuisance uses and uses with air, noise, and light 
emissions have on human health, quality of life, and the environment including prevailing 
meteorological conditions and the potential to transport noxious odors, particulates, and 
emissions.  Special  attention  should  be  paid  to  air  and  water  quality  when  residences, 
schools,  parks,  or  vulnerable  populations  may  be  impacted  and  disadvantaged 
populations  shall  not  be  disproportionately  impacted.  The  subject  EZ-1  properties  are 
concentrated in areas of West Louisville where matters of environmental injustice are well 
documented,  and  residences  have  been  disproportionately  impacted  by  incompatible 
industry prior to and since the inception of zoning regulations. 

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the proposal meets Livability Goal 2 because 
the  Comprehensive  Plan  ensures  equitable  health  and  safety  outcomes  for  all.  The 
proposed  zoning  districts  would  remove  the  potential  for  industrial  redevelopment  on 
properties  enveloped  by  residences.  With  these  changes  new  development  would  be 
encouraged  that  supports  compatible uses  that  do  not  adversely affect  the  health and 
safety outcomes of nearby residences. The existing zoning district would perpetuate the 
inequitable  distribution  of  noxious  industrial  uses  throughout  communities  where 
environmental  injustice  has  negatively  impacted  the  health,  safety,  and  prosperity  of 
residences. 

WHEREAS,  the  Planning  Commission  finds  the  proposal  meets  Housing  Goals  1  &  2 
because the guidelines call for the promotion of housing options and environments that 
support aging in place and locating housing within proximity to multi-modal transportation 
corridors  and  supportive  goods  and  services.  The  proposed  changes  in  zoning  would 
promote development and redevelopment of historic EZ-1 properties for housing within a 
zoning district that is more compatible with residential used based on surrounding land 
uses.  The  subject  properties  are  located  within  established  neighborhoods  with  transit 
routes, access to supportive services, and pedestrian infrastructure. 

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the proposal meets Housing Goal 3, Policy 
2  because  the  Comprehensive  Plan  discourages  displacement  of  existing  residences 
from their communities. The proposal would help to ensure Metro-owned EZ-1 properties 
are not redeveloped for potentially noxious industrial uses that result in adverse impacts 
to existing residences in the community. The proposed zoning changes would promote 
new  residential  housing  that  encourages  residences  to  remain  in  their  communities 
without  the  potential  expansion  of  higher  intensity  land  uses  that  could  result  in 
displacement; now, therefore be it.
