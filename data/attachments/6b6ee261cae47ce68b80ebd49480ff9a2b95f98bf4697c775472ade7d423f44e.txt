CASE NO. 25-ZONE-0042 

Change in zoning from R-4 Single-Family Residential to C-2 Commercial 

On a motion by Commissioner Fischer, seconded by Commissioner Cheek, 
01:23:16 
the  following  resolution, based on  the  staff  report,  the  staff  analysis,  and the  evidence 
and testimony heard today, was adopted:  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Plan 2040 Community 
Form: Goal 1 because the proposal would not represent a non-residential expansion into 
an  existing  residential  area.  The  site  is  located  along  W  Manslick  Road,  a  primary 
collector roadway with a variety of commercial, residential and civic land uses, including 
C-2 Commercial zoning to the immediate south. The proposal is meeting all requirements 
for landscaping and screening, except where relief has been requested and justified for 
a reduction in the width of the buffer along a portion of the property. All required plantings, 
tree  canopy,  and  screening  is  shown  to  be  provided  to  mitigate  any  potential  adverse 
impacts of the proposal on adjacent residences, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
2 because appropriate access and connectivity exist to allow the zoning change within 
the context of the Village Center form district. The property is appropriately located near 
C-2  Commercial  uses  and  north  of  a  main  intersection  in  Fairdale  where  Mt.  Holly, 
Fairdale, and Manslick Roads intersect at the roundabout, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
3  because  the  preliminary  plan  appears  to  be  preserving  natural  resources  on  the 
property where possible. Although the site is not covered in 50 percent or more existing 
tree canopy, the site plan indicates 25,536 sq ft of existing mature tree canopy will be 
preserved. There are no protected waterways on the property and the site is not located 
within the floodplain. Hydric soils are present on the subject site, a wetlands determination 
report was completed and submitted, no jurisdictional wetlands were found on site. LOJIC 
identified potential unstable soils. A geotechnical report was submitted with construction 
recommendations, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
4  because  Historic  Preservation  reviewed  the  proposal  and  determined  no  historic  or 
cultural features would be affected. There is an existing garage on the property that is 
proposed to be removed. Mature tree canopy is being preserved where possible, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Mobility: Goal 1 because 
The site is located along W Manslick Road near existing commercial development and 
employment opportunities. Public transit is not available to the site at this time. However, 
public transit could be extended to the area in the future, and

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Plan 2040 Community 
Form:  Goal  1,  Policy  6  because  the  proposal  would  not  represent  a  non-residential 
expansion into an existing residential area. The site is located along W Manslick Road, a 
primary collector roadway with a variety of commercial, residential and civic land uses, 
including  C-2  Commercial  zoning  to  the  immediate  south.  The  properties  immediately 
south and across the street along W Manslick Road are within the Village Center form 
district, and    

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
2,  Policy  5  because  the  proposed  form  district  would  permit  retail  commercial 
development  in  activity  centers  in  an  area  with  sufficient  population  to  support  the 
proposal, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
2, Policy 6 because the proposed form and zoning district encourage a more compact 
development pattern than the existing R-4 zoning district. C-2 Commercial zoning permits 
a mix of compatible land uses that permit greater density and alternative housing types. 
The Village Center form district encourages a mix of neighborhood serving commercial 
and civic uses, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Mobility Goal 2, Policy 
4  because  the  proposal  does  not  include  access  through  areas  of  significantly  lower 
intensity. W Manslick Road serves a variety of land uses near the subject site that are 
also located within the Village Center form district, now, therefore be it;  

RESOLVED, that the Louisville Metro Planning Commission does hereby RECOMMEND 
that  Louisville  Metro  Council  APPROVE  the  requested  Change  in  form  district  from 
Village-to-Village Center

WHEREAS,  the  Planning  Commission  finds,  the  proposal  is  generally  compliant  with 
most  of  the  applicable  land  use  policies,  objectives,  and  goals  of  the  Comprehensive 
Plan. Community Form Goal 1 Policy 6 calls to ensure an appropriate transition from non-
residential  to  residential  uses  and discourages  nonresidential  expansion to an  existing 
residential area. The proposal would not represent a non-residential expansion into an 
existing residential area. The site is located along W Manslick Road, a primary collector 
roadway  with  a  variety  of  commercial,  residential  and  civic  land  uses,  including  C-2 
Commercial  zoning  to  the  immediate  south.  Adequate  landscape  buffering,  screening, 
and tree canopy is provided as a transition from the adjacent residential development. 
Conversely, the site is located within the boundaries of the Fairdale Neighborhood Plan 
(2006). The Neighborhood Plan states residents want improvements in the design of the 
community through the redevelopment of the village center in Fairdale. At the time of the 
neighborhood plan  adoption,  the  community  sought  changes  in  the  appearance  of  the 
village center with improved store fronts, parking, pedestrian, and vehicular access, traffic 
flow, and the creation of a unique center around a community feature such as a village 
square  or  park.  Further,  continued  development  of  pedestrian  access  and  additional 
housing  opportunities  were  desired  in  the  village  center  (Fairdale  Neighborhood  Plan 
2006, pg. 8). The subject site is not located in the study area for the 2018 Fairdale Village 
Center Plan since the property was not located in Village Center form district at the time 
of the plan, and 

WHEREAS,  the  Planning  Commission  finds,  the  proposal  is  generally  compatible  with 
surrounding  land  uses  and  the  general  character  of  the  area  as  C-2  Commercial  and 
Village Center properties are adjacent to the subject site along Manslick Road, and  

WHEREAS,  the  Planning  Commission  finds,  necessary  on-site  and  off-site  public 
facilities are adequate to serve the proposed use. MSD and Transportation/Public Works 
have reviewed the proposal and given preliminary approval, and
