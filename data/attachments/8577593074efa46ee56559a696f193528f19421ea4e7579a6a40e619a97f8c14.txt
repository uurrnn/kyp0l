its  visibility  from  the  public  right-of-way.  This  condition  differs  from  typical  corner

prominent within the streetscape, and  

WHEREAS, the Development Review Committee finds, the requested waiver does not 
conflict with the Comprehensive Plan. Plan 2040, Community Form Goal 1, Policy 3.1.8 
supports medium- to high-intensity commercial uses within established corridors, and the 
proposal  is  located  along  Dixie  Highway,  an  existing  commercial  corridor.  Community 
Form  Goal  1,  Policy  4  encourages  compatibility  in  scale,  site  design,  and  building 
materials with nearby development. The surrounding area along Dixie Highway contains 
a mix of commercial and residential uses with varied building facade treatments. Many 
nearby commercial properties do not provide 50 percent clear windows and doors along 
street-facing  facades.  In  lieu  of  strictly  meeting  the  transparency  requirement,  the 
applicant proposes enhanced architectural elements, including varied materials, and wall 
projections  consistent  with  the  intent  of  the  Land  Development  Code.  Additionally, 
residential uses along Watson Lane are not located in close proximity to the proposed 
development  area,  and  the  waiver  remains  compatible  with  the  existing  development 
pattern of the corridor, and  

WHEREAS, the Development Review Committee finds, the requested waiver represents 
the  minimum  relief  necessary.  The  facade  facing  Dixie  Highway  continues  to  provide 
windows, with a portion of the glazing being opaque rather than clear, partially satisfying 
the intent of the Land Development Code. The Watson Lane facade serves the drive-thru 
and is not typically designed with 50 percent clear windows. It is also located more than 
250 feet from the property line, substantially limiting its visibility from the public right-of-
way compared to typical corner developments, and

are within the 80-90% total parking occupancy for the peak demand of 196 spaces, now, 
therefore be it;
