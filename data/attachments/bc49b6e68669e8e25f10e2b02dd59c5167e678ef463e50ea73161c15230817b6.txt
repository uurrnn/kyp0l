00:08:32 
On a motion by Commissioner Steff, seconded by Commissioner Benitez, 
the following resolution, based on the staff report, staff analysis, and the evidence and 
testimony heard today, was adopted:  

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  that the  requested  subdivision 
will  not  conflict  with  the  established  pattern  in  the  neighborhood  and  will  promote  the 
public  health,  safety,  or  welfare  by  facilitating  development  or  rehabilitation  of  such 
property compatible with the surrounding neighborhood, and  

WHEREAS, the Development Review Committee finds, the proposed substandard minor 
subdivision will not conflict with the established pattern in the neighborhood, given that 
the Traditional Neighborhood form district is characterized by predominantly narrow and 
deep  lots,  and  that  the  existing  conditions  for  the  surrounding  area  also  include 
substandard  sized  lots.  Although  currently  treated  as  an  alley  in  the  current  lot 
configuration,  Fern  Street  is  a  local  level  road  that  has  dwelling  units  on  substandard 
sized lots that front it for much of its length. Examples of substandard lots exist within the 
1st and 2nd tier adjoining properties, such as 1224 Lexington Rd and 1225 Fern St, now, 
therefore be it;  

RESOLVED,  that  the  Louisville  Metro  Development  Review  Committee  does  hereby 
APPROVE the requested Minor Subdivision to create two (2) substandard lots pursuant 
to Land Development Code (LDC) Section 7.1.85  

The vote was as follows: 

YES: Commissioners Lannert, Stuber, Benitez, Steff, Fischer
