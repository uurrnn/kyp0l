02:44:11 
On  a  motion  by  Commissioner  Lannert,  seconded  by  Commissioner 
Fischer,  the  following  resolution,  based  on  the  staff  report,  the  staff  analysis,  and  the 
evidence and testimony heard today, was adopted:  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the proposal meets Plan 2040 Community 
Form: Goal 1 because the proposal would not constitute non-residential expansion into 
an existing residential area since there is a mix of residential and non-residential zoning 
and uses within adjacent to the site. The applicant shall demonstrate that any adverse 
impacts  on  residential  uses  will  be  mitigated.  Landscaping  and  screening  shall  be 
provided, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
2 because appropriate access and connectivity exist to allow the zoning change within 
the context of the Neighborhood form district. The subject site is located along an arterial 
roadway and developing corridor with a variety of residential densities and commercial 
zoning districts within the immediate area. There is connectivity to the I-265 Expressway, 
and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
3 because no wet or highly permeable soils, severe, steep or unstable slopes appear to 
be present on site. Sanitary sewers and drainage will be provided as required by MSD, 
and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Community Form: Goal 
4 there are no known historic or cultural features on site. There is existing mature tree 
canopy. The proposal shall be required with comply with all tree canopy and landscaping 
requirements, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds, the proposal meets Mobility: Goal 1 because 
the proposed district is located along Taylorsville Rd, a major arterial roadway with I-265 
connectivity. The site is located near employment centers, parks, and similar services and 
amenities as permitted in the requested district. Public transit serves are not available at 
this location. However, public transit routes could be expanded to the area, and

evidence and testimony heard today, was adopted:  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the waiver will not adversely affect adjacent 
property  owners  since  the proposed  building  is  setback  approximately  425  ft.  from  the 
front  property  line  along  Taylorsville  Road.  Also,  there  is  required  landscape  buffering 
and screening that separates the proposed buildings from adjacent properties of lower 
intensity, and    

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the wavier will not violate specific guidelines 
of Plan 2040. Community Form Goal 1, Policy 9 calls to ensure an appropriate transition 
between  uses  that  are  substantially  different  in  scale  and  intensity  or  density  of 
development.  The  transition  may  be  achieved  through  methods  such  as  landscaped 
buffer  yards,  vegetative  berms,  compatible  building  design  and  materials,  height 
restrictions  and  setback  requirements.  The  proposal  is  compliant  with  all  landscaping 
buffer area and tree canopy requirements in LDC Chapter 10. Adequate landscaping and 
buffering are provided along the Taylorsville Road frontage and the proposed building is 
setback approximately 425 ft. thereby lessening the impact of the primary façade on the 
street.  A  4  ft.  berm  and  15  ft.  landscape  buffer  area  are  shown  on  the  plan  along  the 
frontage of the property. The proposed building is meeting requirements in LDC 5.6 for 
building  design  with  respect  to  the  varying  roof  line  and  animating  features  along  the 
façade, and  

WHEREAS, the Planning Commission finds the extent of the waiver of the regulation is 
not the minimum necessary to afford relief to the applicant since the building could be
