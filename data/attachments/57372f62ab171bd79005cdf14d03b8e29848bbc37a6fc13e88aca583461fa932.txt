Waiver  of  Land  Development  Code  (LDC)  Section  8.2.1.D.6  to  allow  a  changing 
image sign to be closer than 300 feet to a residentially used property.  

00:12:40 
On a motion by Commissioner Steff, seconded by Commissioner Mims, the 
following  resolution,  based  on  the  staff  report,  the  staff  analysis,  the  evidence  and 
testimony heard today, was adopted: 

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  waiver  will  not  adversely 
affect adjacent property owners or residents as the sign is at least 150 ft away from all 
residences, will be perpendicular to the two closest residences, and will have automatic 
dimming features, and  

WHEREAS, the Development Review Committee finds, Community Form Goal 1, Policy 
14  calls  for  ensuring  that  signs  are  compatible  with  the  Form  District  pattern  and 
contribute to the visual quality of their surroundings, as well as promoting signs of a size 
and height adequate for effective communication and conducive to motor vehicle safety. 
The waiver will not violate this specific guideline of Plan 2040 as the sign is well under 
the  maximum  size  and  height  for  signs  in  the  Suburban  Workplace  form  district. 
Additionally, the proposed sign calls for a reduction in square footage from the existing 
sign  by  nearly  50%,  which  helps  minimize  adverse  impacts  on  motor  vehicle  safety. 
Community Form Goal 1, Policy 14 additionally encourages signs that are integrated with 
existing structures and encourages limiting freestanding signs to monument style signs 
unless  such  design  would  unreasonably  compromise  sign  effectiveness.  The  proposal 
will violate this specific section of the guideline, as the proposal calls for the removal of 
an existing monument sign in favor of a columnar sign, and  

WHEREAS, the Development Review Committee finds, the requested waiver represents 
the minimum extent necessary to provide relief to the applicant, as there are no locations 
on the site that would allow the sign to be seen from the road while complying with the 
300’ minimum regulation, and

Waiver from St. Matthews Development Code Article 12 D1.(a) to omit the property 
perimeter landscape buffer required along the southern property line (26-WAIVER-
0034)  

00:23:50 
On a motion by Commissioner Mims, seconded by Commissioner Steff, the 
following resolution, based on the staff report, the staff analysis, and the evidence and 
testimony heard today, was adopted:  

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  requested  waiver  will  not 
adversely affect adjacent property owners, as the patio is located on the corner portion of 
the  site  and  does  not  introduce  additional  intensity  or  encroach  upon  neighboring 
properties.  The  site  maintains  separation  from  adjacent  uses  through  existing  right-of-
way, sidewalks, and open space areas, and the patio functions as a low-impact accessory 
feature, and  

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  requested  waiver  will  not 
violate specific guidelines of Plan 2040. The waiver will not violate the Comprehensive 
Plan, as the request is limited in scope and supports the continued use of the site in a 
manner  consistent  with  commercial  development  patterns.  Community  Form  Goal  1, 
Policy 3.1.5 encourages a compact mixture of moderately intense uses within the Town 
Center form, including shopping, restaurants, offices, and residential uses. The proposed 
patio represents a minor modification to a previously approved development and supports 
the  continued  use  of  the  site  for  office-related  activities.  The  addition  enhances  the 
functionality of the property while remaining consistent with the established development 
pattern  and  maintaining  compliance  with  applicable  site  design  standards,  with  the 
exception of the requested waiver, and  

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee finds,  the extent  of  the  waiver  is  the 
minimum necessary to afford relief to the applicant, as it applies only to the portion of the 
site  where  the  patio  encroaches  into  the  required  LBA.  The  remainder  of  the  site 
continues  to  comply  with  applicable  landscaping  requirements,  and  no  additional 
encroachments are proposed, and
