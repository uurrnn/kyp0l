LIMESTONE CLIFF FARM-UPDATE 

Request:           
Project Name:   
Case Manager:  

Limestone Cliff Farm-Update 
Limestone Cliff Farm-Update 
Kurt Mason  

Kurt Mason noted that the $21,994.50 donation came from Limestone Cliff 
01:17:36 
Farm  Preservation  and  was  given  to  LJCET  for  the  stewardship  of  the  conservation 
easement.  Mason  noted  that  as  part  of  stewardship,  donations  may  be  requested; 
however,  whether  or  not  the  conservation  easement  owner  chooses  to  donate  is  not 
grounds  for  rejecting  the  easement.  Mason  explained  that  a  stewardship  calculator  is 
used to determine the donation amount and that the process has two components. The 
first  component  involves  the  total  value  of  the  conservation  easement  in  terms  of  its 
ecological  benefit.  Part  of  that  includes  its  ecological  value  to  Louisville  Metro,  which 
discounts  the  stewardship  request.  Mason  noted  that  a  letter  had  been  sent 
acknowledging the contribution to LJCET and indicating that the contribution was not in 
exchange for something of value, which means that the landowner could use it as a tax 
deduction. He also stated that a second letter acknowledging the gift to the conservation 
easement  had  not  yet  been  sent.  Mason  mentioned  that  the  donation  material  was 
recorded in Oldham County and then the original copy will be sent back to Anne Scholtz 
in Jefferson County (see recording for details).
