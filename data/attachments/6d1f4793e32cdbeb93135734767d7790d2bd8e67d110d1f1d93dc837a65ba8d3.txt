Waiver  from  Land  Development  Code  Section  5.4.2.C.1  to  allow  an  accessory 
structure to exceed the building footprint of a primary structure.  

00:17:44 
On a motion by Commissioner Steff, seconded by Commissioner Lannert, 
the following resolution, based on the staff report, staff analysis, and the evidence and 
testimony heard today, was adopted: 

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  proposed  waiver  will  not 
adversely affect adjacent property owners. The accessory structure is screened from view 
from all adjacent properties by existing trees and is not visible from Cedar Creek Rd, and 

WHEREAS, the Development Review Committee finds, Community Form Goal 1, Policy 
4  calls  for ensuring  new  development  and  redevelopment  is  compatible  with  the  scale 
and  site  design  of  nearby  existing  development  and  with  the  desired  pattern  of 
development within the Form District, while using quality design and building materials. 
The  proposed  layout  is  consistent  with  this  established  development  pattern  in  the 
Neighborhood Form, and 

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  extent  of  the  requested 
waiver  represents  the  minimum  necessary  relief  for  the  applicant,  as  the  structure  is 
existing and cannot easily be removed or reduced in size, and 

WHEREAS, the Development Review Committee finds, strict application of the provisions 
of the regulations would deprive the applicant of the reasonable use of the property and 
create  an  unnecessary  hardship.  The  site  is  completely  screened  from  view  and  the 
proposal  will  have  no  adverse  effects  on  any  neighboring  property.  Additionally,  the 
structure is existing, and the size cannot be reduced to meet the regulation, now, therefore 
be it;  

RESOLVED,  that  the  Louisville  Metro  Development  Review  Committee  does  hereby 
APPROVE  the  requested  Waiver  from  Land  Development  Code  Section  5.4.2.C.1  to 
allow an accessory structure to exceed the building footprint of a primary structure.

00:28:15 
following  resolution,  based  on  the  staff  report,  staff  analysis,  and  the  evidence  and 
testimony heard today, was adopted: 

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  Waiver  will  not  adversely 
affect  adjacent  property  owners  as  it  is  internal  to  the  site  and  reflects  the  pattern  of 
commercial development to the south on Terra Crossing Boulevard, and  

WHEREAS, the Development Review Committee finds, the Waiver will not violate specific 
guidelines of the Comprehensive Plan. Plan 2040 Community Form Goal 1, Policy 3.1.10 
characterizes the Suburban Workplace form district as predominantly industrial and office 
uses where the buildings are set back from the street in a landscaped setting. Community 
Form  Goal  1,  Policy  4  calls  to  ensure  new  development  and  redevelopment  are 
compatible with the scale and site design of nearby existing development within the Form 
District.  Quality  design  and  building  materials  should  be  promoted  to  enhance 
compatibility  of  development  and  redevelopment  projects.  Community  Form  Goal  1, 
Policy 11 seeks to ensure setbacks, lot dimensions and building heights are compatible 
with  those  of  nearby  developments  that  meet  Form  District  guidelines.  The  existing 
development pattern on Terra Crossing Blvd features a variety of building scales from 1-
to-4 story commercial, office, and institutional uses with brick, stucco, and numerous other 
façade materials. Setbacks vary widely on the Terra Crossing Blvd frontage. The proposal 
incorporates  similar  building  materials,  appropriate  scale,  and  adequate  setbacks  that 
meet  the  LDC,  supporting  compatibility  with  the  surrounding  area.  Commercial 
developments  to  the  south  of  the  subject  site  generally  do  not  appear  to  feature  clear 
windows and doors for more than 50% of the facade facing Terra Crossing Blvd; in lieu 
of  this  requirement,  the  applicant  has  supplemented  the  proposed  windows  and 
entrances with greater architectural interest than required by the LDC in terms of building 
materials, wall projections, roof heights, and additional animating details, and
