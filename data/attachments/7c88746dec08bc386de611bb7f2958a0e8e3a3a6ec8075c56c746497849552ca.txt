WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  requested  waiver  will  not 
adversely  affect  adjacent  property  owners,  as  the  plan  will  still  provide  a  35-foot 
landscape buffer with the full required plantings along the shared property line with the 
adjacent  townhomes.  In  addition,  truck  loading  areas  are  oriented  away  from  the 
residential  property,  and  the  maximum  building  height  near  the  shared  boundary  is 
approximately 41 feet, which is lower than the adjacent townhomes at approximately 45 
feet. The full 50-foot buffer is maintained along the northeast portion of the site, where 
circulation  does  not  occur,  ensuring  that  substantial  buffering  remains  across  the 
development, and  

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  requested  waiver  will  not 
violate specific guidelines of Plan 2040. Community Form Goal 1, Policy 9 encourages 
appropriate  transitions  between  uses  that  differ  in  scale,  intensity,  or  density  through 
measures  such  as  landscaped  buffer  yards,  vegetative  berms,  compatible  building 
design,  height  restrictions,  and  setback  requirements.  The  proposed  design  maintains 
this  transition  by  providing  a  35-foot  landscaped  buffer  with  the  full  required  plantings 
between  the  industrial  warehouse  and  the  adjacent  townhomes,  while  orienting 
operational elements away from the residential property. This configuration continues to 
provide effective screening and supports compatibility between the differing land uses, 
and  

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  extent  of  the  requested 
waiver  is  the  minimum  necessary  to  afford  relief  to  the  applicant,  as  the  buffer  is  only 
reduced  from  50  feet  to  35  feet  along  the  portion  of  the  site  needed  to  accommodate 
vehicular  circulation  around  the  warehouse,  while  the  full  50-foot  buffer  is  maintained 
along other portions of the shared boundary, and  

WHEREAS,  the  Development  Review  Committee  finds,  the  strict  application  of  the 
provisions of the regulation would create an unnecessary hardship on the applicant, as 
the 50-foot buffer would limit the available developable area needed to provide necessary 
vehicular  circulation  around  the  warehouse  building,  which  is  required  to  support  the 
operational needs of the proposed industrial use, now, therefore be it;  

RESOLVED,  that  the  Louisville  Metro  Development  Review  Committee  does  hereby 
APPROVE the requested Waiver of Land Development Code Section 5.5.4.B.1 to reduce 
the 50-foot landscape buffer area along the eastern property line. (26-WAIVER-0012)
